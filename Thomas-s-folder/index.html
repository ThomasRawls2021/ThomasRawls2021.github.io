Information on Cholera

About Cholera

Cholera is an infectious disease of the small intestine that severe vomiting and diarrhea. Cholera is cause by the bacteria Vibrio cholera. A person can be infected by drinking water or eating food that is contaminated with the cholera bacteria. Countries affected by war, poverty, and natural disasters have the greatest risk for a cholera outbreak. It is mostly found in Africa, the Caribbean Islands, Asia, Latin America, India, and the middle east. It is rare in the U.S.Every year 3-5 million people around the world are infected with cholera and 100,000- 120,000 people die from the infectious disease, according to estimates by the World Health Organization (WHO).

Symptoms
  • Diarrhea.
  • Nausea and vomiting.
  • Dehydration.
  • Electrolyte imbalance
  • Muscle cramps
  • Shock.
    • Signs of cholera in children
  • An altered state of consciousness
  • Seizures
  • Coma
Statistics

Cholera is often found in the tropic areas such as Asia, Africa, India, and Latin America. The largest cholera outbreak that occured was in Yemen in December of 20 17. There were over one million cases and 2,227 people died from the disease
